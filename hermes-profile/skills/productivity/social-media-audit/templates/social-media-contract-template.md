# Social Media Management Contract Template

Use this template when a client wants to move from audit → proposal → contract. Customize per client, but keep the structure. Every section exists for a reason — learned over 8+ rounds of iteration with a real client (AtllasAI, May 2026).

---

## Template Structure

```
1. Parties & Term
2. Scope of Work (primary platform, add-ons)
3. Brand Voice (channel client's existing founder/company energy)
4. Content Mix & Storytelling Commitment (pillars + % + weekly calendar)
5. Deliverables & Approval Cadence (trust model on fast content)
6. Tools & Subscriptions (client provisions: CapCut, Canva, DaVinci, AuditLayer)
7. Performance Metrics (KPIs with baseline → target)
8. Pricing (tiered: Core / Growth / Full-Stack + setup fee + travel stipend)
9. Payment Terms + Non-Payment Pause
10. Non-Exclusivity
11. Client Responsibilities
12. Content Ownership & Licensing + Raw Footage Rights
13. Confidentiality
14. Termination + Kill Fee
15. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution
16. Limitation of Liability
17. What Makes This Different (proposal cover page material)
```

## Key Design Decisions (Don't Change These)

### Reel Count: 3-4/week, not 5
Quality storytelling beats volume. Every Reel needs a hero → villain → transformation arc. More than 4/week becomes a content treadmill. Less than 3/week doesn't build algorithmic momentum. 3-4 is the sweet spot.

### No-Approval on Reels & Stories
Speed is a competitive advantage. Approval bottlenecks kill the raw, documentary energy that makes founder-led accounts work. Carousels (permanent feed posts) get a light review. Two revision rounds max.

### Kill Fee: 50% of Remaining Term
If client cancels before the 90-day minimum, they pay 50% of remaining months. This covers strategy investment and ramp-up. Standard for creative retainers.

### Non-Payment Pause: Immediate
If payment isn't received by Net 15, pause ALL work immediately. Content, posting, community — everything stops. Paused days don't extend the contract. Never work for free.

### Raw Footage: Yours Until Paid
You own all unedited footage from on-site shoots until payment clears. On termination: client owns posted content; you transfer or delete raw footage within 30 days. They can't keep raw footage from unpaid periods.

### Travel Stipend: Built Into Monthly Invoice
If you're doing weekly on-site shoots, add a flat travel stipend (~$200/mo for a ~50-mile round trip). Built into the monthly invoice, not a separate bill. They see it once and forget about it.

### AuditLayer: Paid Line Item
AuditLayer is a paid tool, not "included." Client covers the subscription as part of Tools & Subscriptions.

### Non-Exclusivity: Explicit
You're building an agency, not taking a job. You're free to work with other clients — including in adjacent industries. Give a heads-up if a direct competitor approaches you.

## Content Mix Defaults

Start with these percentages and adjust per client:

| Pillar | % | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Product in Action | 35-40% | The product as sidekick, not hero. Documentary, not demo. |
| Customer Transformations | 20-25% | Before/after stories. Real people, real numbers. |
| Industry Narrative | 25-30% | Positioning the company as inevitable. Meme/voiceover format. |
| Founder / Building in Public | 10-15% | Raw, personal, founder energy. |

**The rule:** Every post needs a hero, a villain, and a transformation. If it doesn't have all three, it doesn't ship.

## Content Ecosystem (Multi-Person Accounts)

When the account features a team (not just a solo founder):

| Role | Screen Time | Function |
|---|---|---|
| CEO/Founder | 60% | Main face. Founder energy. The guy. |
| Team | 25% | Engineers, sales, support. Real humans behind the product. |
| Customers | 15% | Testimonials. Transformations. They're the heroes. |

Weekly on-site shoot: capture raw footage of everyone. This is the content factory.

## Pricing Benchmarks (2026)

| Tier | Range | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| Core | $2,000-3,000/mo | Strategy + calendar + 3-4 Reels + 1 carousel/week + captions + hashtags |
| Growth | $3,500-5,000/mo | Core + daily Stories + community management + monthly reports |
| Full-Stack | $5,000-8,000/mo | Growth + cross-platform management + founder content + quarterly audits |

Setup fee: $1,500-2,500 one-time.

## The Closer

Lead with the audit. No other social media person leads with an audit. You found everything broken before signing. That's the proposal.

Frame yourself as a **storytelling strategist with competitive intelligence infrastructure** — not a social media manager. The audit, the storytelling framework, and the quarterly competitor monitoring are your moat.

The before/after table (current state → your plan) goes on the proposal cover page. Make it impossible to say no.
