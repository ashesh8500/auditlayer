# Social Media A/B Testing Playbook

## The Core Rule
Change **exactly ONE variable per test.** If you change the hook *and* the thumbnail, you'll never know which one moved the needle. This is where most people fail — they tweak everything and learn nothing.

## What to Test

### Hooks (first 1–3 seconds)
- Question vs. bold statement ("Have you ever wondered why..." vs "This one thing is destroying your sleep")
- Emotional opener vs. factual opener
- Face-first vs. visual-first opening frame
- Surprising result first vs. setup-then-reveal

### Thumbnails
- Face vs. no face
- Text overlay vs. no text
- Close-up vs. wide shot
- Bright/contrasting background vs. muted/clean
- Polished/professional vs. raw/screenshot (for science/education, the "imperfect" one often wins — audiences trust it more)

### Captions
- Short & punchy (2-3 lines) vs. story-driven (8+ lines)
- Question ending vs. statement ending
- CTA type: "save this" vs. "share this" vs. "comment below"
- Emoji-heavy vs. no emojis

### Science/Health Niche Quick Wins
- Thumbnails: face + provocative one-liner almost always beats visual/diagram for personal-brand science content
- Hooks: lead with the surprising result first, not the setup
- Captions: reverse-engineer the account's viral posts — find the pattern and use it as Variant A for all new content

## How to Run Tests

### Instagram/TikTok (no native A/B tool for organic)
1. Post Variant A on Tuesday at normal time
2. Post Variant B on Thursday at the *same time*, identical everything except the one variable
3. Let each run for **minimum 72 hours** before comparing
4. Track in a spreadsheet — never in your head

### YouTube
- Use YouTube Studio → Experiments (built-in)
- Upload 2-3 thumbnails, YouTube auto-splits impressions
- Wait for 1,000+ impressions per variant before declaring a winner

### Twitter/X
- Alternate posting times but keep day-of-week consistent
- 48 hours is enough for most accounts

## The Tracking Sheet

Minimum columns: Date | Platform | Variable Tested | Variant A | Variant B | Winner | Key Metric | Notes

After 10-15 tests, patterns emerge. You stop guessing and start knowing what the audience responds to.

## Testing Cadence
- Run **2 structured tests per week**
- Test hooks first (highest leverage), then thumbnails, then captions
- Double down on winners, kill losers fast
- Build a "proven playbook" from 12+ tests for all future content

## Duration & Sample Size
- Instagram/Facebook: minimum 7 days
- LinkedIn: 2 weeks
- Twitter: 2-4 days
- Minimum impressions per variant: 1,000+ on YouTube, a few hundred for smaller accounts
- Avoid testing during outlier days (holidays, major news events)
