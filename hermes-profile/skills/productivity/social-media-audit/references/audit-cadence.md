# Audit Cadence Section

Template for the "How Often Should You Re-Audit?" section. Place after the Road to [Milestone] section and before the footer. This gives the report a forward-looking closing — when to come back and check progress.

Every report MUST include all four cadences: weekly, monthly, quarterly, and after a good performing post.

## Template

```html
<!-- AUDIT CADENCE -->
<section>

  <p>An audit isn't a one-time event — it's a recurring checkpoint. Here's the recommended cadence for an account at this tier:</p>

  <table class="data-table">

    </thead>
```

## Placement
Default: After the Road to [Milestone] section, before the footer — the final substantive section.

Alternatively, place after the footer (below the disclaimer line) when the user wants it as a true appendix — the very last thing on the page. This signals the audit cadence is operational housekeeping, not part of the main argument. Ask the user or default to before-footer unless they specify otherwise.

## Cadence principles
- **Weekly**: 10 minutes. Catch problems before they become patterns. The compounding cadence.
- **Monthly**: Not a report — a dashboard glance. 30 minutes. Catches problems before the quarter ends.
- **Quarterly**: The real re-audit. Full metrics comparison, updated peers, progress against the roadmap. 2–3 hours.
- **After a good performing post**: The highest-leverage check. When a post hits 2×+ your average, audit it immediately. The algorithm surfaced you to a new audience — figure out why and replicate within 48 hours. These asymmetric checks often teach more than all the scheduled ones combined.
