# Multi-Handle Brand — Dormant Domain-Matched Placeholder

## Detection Signature

All of the following must be true:

1. The audited Instagram handle exactly matches the brand's primary domain (e.g., @eatfishwife ↔ eatfishwife.com)
2. The handle is dormant: ≤50 followers, ≤5 posts, no bio, no highlights, no link
3. The brand operates a SECOND, active Instagram handle under a different name (e.g., @fishwife at 288K)
4. The brand has cross-platform presence under the dormant handle's name (e.g., TikTok @eatfishwife at 146.6K)
5. The brand itself is established — revenue, press, retail distribution, or funding all confirmed

**This is NOT a pre-launch, test/spoof, or walled-garden account.** The brand behind the handle is real and successful. The handle was registered defensively and never activated. Only the metadata is sparse.

## How to Frame the Audit

The audit is an **activation playbook**, not an optimization report and not a brand autopsy. The framing must acknowledge:

- The handle IS a brand asset — domain-matched, cross-platform-consistent, defensively valuable
- The brand behind it is successful — the audit is not questioning product, positioning, or business model
- The dormant state is a brand-impression liability, not a neutral state
- The content engine already exists — the challenge is deployment, not creation

**Opening paragraph template:**
> "@handle is a dormant placeholder handle — and that is the finding. The Instagram account matching [Brand]'s domain name ([domain]) has [N] followers, [N] accounts followed, and [N] posts. It has never been activated. Meanwhile, [Brand] operates [description of active presence — @mainhandle at X followers, TikTok at Y followers, etc.]. This audit is not a post-mortem of an underperforming account — it is an activation playbook for a brand asset that has been reserved but never deployed."

## Handle Strategy Decision (Mandatory Section)

Every multi-handle dormant audit must present three strategy options in order of effort:

1. **Redirect** — Bio: "Our main account is @mainhandle →", one post. Lowest effort, solves the dead-end immediately. Best when: the brand doesn't need a second handle, just wants to close the impression gap.
2. **Replicate** — Mirror top @mainhandle content with a short delay, cross-link bios. Doubles surface area at near-zero content cost. Best when: the brand wants maximum search coverage with minimal additional content investment.
3. **Differentiate** — Position the dormant handle as a distinct content hub (founder voice, recipes, BTS, education) while the main handle remains the flagship. Highest effort, highest ceiling. Best when: the brand has enough content variety to sustain two distinct identities.

The report must state a recommended option with rationale. Do not leave this as an open question.

## Scoring Calibration

Score the HANDLE, not the brand. The overall score will be low (8–15/100) because the handle has done nothing. This must be stated explicitly — otherwise the score reads as an insult to a successful brand.

> "This score reflects the handle's current state — not the brand behind it. [Brand] as a company would score [estimate]/100 on its active @mainhandle presence. But @dormanhandle specifically is a dormant asset with near-zero activation."

The standout dimension is always **Growth Potential** — because the brand equity, content library, and existing audience provide an acceleration mechanism most dormant accounts lack.

## Peer Comparison Strategy

Same-tier peers (0–50 followers) in the specific niche almost never exist in public indices. The category's operating brands cluster at higher tiers.

**Approved approach:** Use 3 verified off-tier peers — the smallest active brands in the same category. State follower counts honestly. Add a callout explaining why off-tier peers are used:

> "Same-tier peers (0–50 followers) in [category] do not exist in public indices — the category's smallest operating brands cluster at [X]K+ followers due to [reason]. The accounts below are verified off-tier peers: the smallest active [category] brands with public Instagram presences. They represent the activation baseline @handle should surpass within 90 days given [Brand]'s existing brand equity."

## Cross-Promotion as the 1% Compounding Move

The single highest-leverage action in every multi-handle dormant audit is the cross-promotion bridge. The brand already owns an audience at @mainhandle. One Story per week = thousands of potential followers.

Frame this as the 1% Compounding Move:
> "One @mainhandle Story per week directing followers to @dormanhandle. At [N]K followers, even a 0.1% Story conversion rate = [N] new followers per week. That single action, done consistently for 12 weeks, would deliver ~[N] followers — [X]× the 90-day target. No ad spend, no new content, no algorithm dependency."

## Pitfalls

- **Do NOT frame as brand failure.** The brand is successful elsewhere. The audit diagnoses a handle-specific issue, not a brand-wide one.
- **Do NOT suggest abandoning the handle.** Domain-matched handles are defensively valuable even if never activated — a redirect bio is the minimum viable activation.
- **Do NOT use standard dormant scoring (0–2/100).** A domain-matched handle for an established brand has more inherent value than a random dormant placeholder. Score 8–15/100.
- **Do NOT fabricate same-tier peers.** The structural absence of 0–50-follower brands in mature CPG categories is a finding, not a research failure.
- **Verify the relationship between handles before publishing.** Confirm the dormant handle and the active handle belong to the same brand. Check the domain, check cross-platform handles, check the bio/link on the active account.
