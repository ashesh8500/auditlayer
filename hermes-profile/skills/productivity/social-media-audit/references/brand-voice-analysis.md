# Brand Voice Analysis from Founder Accounts

When auditing a company account where the founder maintains a personal brand on social media, study the founder's accounts to extract the vibe — then apply it to the company account. This turns a generic brand account into an extension of the founder's energy.

## Methodology

1. **Identify founder accounts** — Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, YouTube, LinkedIn
2. **Extract bio + follower data** — understand their positioning and audience size
3. **Analyze content patterns** — what formats do they use? What's the emotional range?
4. **Extract voice attributes** — documentary? polished? vulnerable? aggressive? humorous?
5. **Map to company account** — which attributes translate? Which don't?
6. **Define the rule** — a single sentence that governs all content decisions

## Case Study: @neema_bardi → @atllasai

**Founder:** Nima Bardi
- Instagram: @neema_bardi — 10K followers, "Filming my life until my startup hits $1M/month"
- TikTok: @neemabardi — 8.8K followers, 105.6K likes, same bio + "Founder AtllasX AI Calling"

**Voice Attributes Extracted:**
- **Documentary, not commercial** — "Filming my life 24/7" energy, raw and unpolished
- **Building in public** — late nights, product updates, customer wins, struggles — all of it
- **Fast-paced editing** — CapCut style, quick cuts, text overlays, trending audio, meme energy
- **Vulnerability** — "Probably the most vulnerable thing I've ever done." Not just wins.
- **Voiceover narration** — first-person storytelling via voice clone or real voice
- **Reaction content** — customer DMs, competitor claims, industry news — reacted to, not just reported
- **Fitness/lifestyle woven in** — startup grind + personal life. The whole founder, not just the CEO
- **Mid-20s energy** — not corporate, not polished, not trying to be something he's not
- **"The long way is the shortcut"** — grind mentality, endurance, authenticity over polish

**Translation to @atllasai:**
- Product demos → shot documentary-style, not as polished feature walkthroughs
- Agent stories → reaction-format, not case study slides
- Industry content → meme/green-screen energy, not LinkedIn thought leadership
- Founder content → voice clone narration, behind-the-scenes, "here's what we shipped"

**The Rule:** "If it feels like a polished SaaS ad, kill it. If it feels like something Nima would post on @neema_bardi, ship it."

## Voice Attributes Taxonomy

When analyzing founder accounts, classify attributes into these categories:

| Category | Question to Ask |
|---|---|
| **Production style** | Polished or raw? Studio or phone camera? Scripted or improvised? |
| **Emotional range** | Wins only or struggles too? Vulnerable or guarded? |
| **Pacing** | Fast cuts or long takes? Trending audio or original sound? |
| **Narration** | First-person voiceover? Text-only? Talking head? Voice clone? |
| **Content type** | Original? Reaction? Documentary? Tutorial? Meme? |
| **Personal/professional mix** | All business or whole person? Hobbies visible? |
| **Audience relationship** | Broadcast or conversation? Replies to comments? DMs shared? |
| **Age/energy signal** | Corporate/professional or young/startup? What generation does it speak to? |

## When Brand Voice Analysis Applies

- The founder has an active personal account with a distinct vibe
- The company account feels disconnected from the founder's energy
- The audit identifies "no brand voice" as a weakness
- The target audience overlaps with the founder's personal audience

## When It Doesn't

- The founder has no personal social presence
- The founder's personal brand would hurt the company brand (controversial, off-topic)
- The company is enterprise/corporate and needs a separate institutional voice
