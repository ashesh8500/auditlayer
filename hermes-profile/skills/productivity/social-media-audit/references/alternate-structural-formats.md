# Alternate Structural Formats — Full Section Templates

Three proven frameworks for portfolio variety. All use the same visual layer (Hemal light theme: teal accent, Inter + JetBrains Mono, `--bg: #fafaf9`). What changes is how information is organized and framed.

---

## 1. Competitive Battlefield (brand/company accounts)

**Tone:** Tactical, competitive, urgent. Military/competitive language.  
**Best for:** Brands fighting in crowded categories, product-credibility-high/content-operations-weak accounts.

```
1. The Battlefield (competitive landscape + stakes)
2. The Arsenal — What [Brand] Brings to the Fight (strengths as weapons)
3. The Blind Spots — Where [Brand] is Taking Fire (weaknesses as vulnerabilities)
4. The Opponent's Moves — How Competitors Are Fighting (peer comparison)
5. Battlefield Map — Where the Algorithm Rewards and Punishes (signal diagnostics)
6. The Campaign — How [Brand] Wins This Battlefield (5-move growth strategy)
7. Victory Conditions — What Winning Looks Like (benchmarks)
8. 90-Day Campaign — Phase by Phase (Mobilize → Advance → Dominate)
9. Battlefield Reassessment Cadence
```

Proven on: @discovermescreen (May 2026)

## 2. Founder Story Arc (personal-brand/CEO accounts)

**Tone:** Editorial, intimate, story-driven. Narrative language.  
**Best for:** Founders, CEOs, personal brands with rich backstories not in their feed.

```
1. The Chapter Being Written (situation as narrative hook)
2. Who He Is — The Material (strengths as character backstory)
3. The Gap — Between the Man and the Feed (root cause as narrative tension)
4. What His Peers Are Publishing — The Genre Context (peer comparison)
5. The Stories Not Yet Told (weaknesses as untold chapters)
6. The Storytelling Engine — How to Tell It (growth strategy as narrative modes)
7. This Week — Start Telling the Story (quick wins)
8. The Next Chapters — 90-Day Narrative Timeline (Prologue → Rising Action → Climax)
9. Chapter Milestones (benchmarks)
10. When to Re-Read the Story (audit cadence)
```

Proven on: @sean.fetcho (May 2026)

## 3. Research Translation Scorecard (academic/researcher accounts)

**Tone:** Analytical, evidence-based, clinical. Research/lab language.  
**Best for:** Academics, PhDs, researchers, scientists with deep publications but underperforming social presence.

```
1. The Investigator — What the Lab Knows (credentials + research depth)
2. The Translation Gap — Bench → Feed (metrics + gap analysis)
3. What Translates Well — Credibility Assets on the Feed (strengths)
4. What Doesn't Translate — Where the Signal Drops (weaknesses as signal loss)
5. The Publication Portfolio vs. The Content Feed (root cause table)
6. Peer Review — How Comparable Investigators Are Performing (peer comparison)
7. The Engagement Assay — Signal by Signal (algorithm diagnostics)
8. Protocol Optimization — Translating the Research for the Platform (growth strategy)
9. Immediate Actions — This Week (quick wins)
10. Clinical Trial Milestones — 90-Day Protocol (Baseline → Dose Escalation → Compound)
11. Scorecard Milestones (benchmarks)
12. Re-Assessment Cadence
```

Proven on: @hemalpatelphd (May 2026)

## 4. Brand Pulse Report (CPG/food/consumer brand accounts)

**Tone:** Consumer-aware, shelf-to-screen framing. Retail + product language.  
**Best for:** CPG brands, food/snack companies, DTC consumer products with retail distribution whose Instagram lags behind their shelf presence.

```
1. The Product — What's in the [Product] (product truth, formulation, retail credibility)
2. The Story — The Best Ingredient They're Not Posting (founding narrative, cultural IP, social impact)
3. The Shelf vs. The Feed — Where the Brand Lives (metrics + gap analysis table)
4. What's Working — Brand Assets on the Feed (strengths)
5. What's Missing — Content White Space (weaknesses as untapped content opportunities)
6. The Competitive Shelf — Peer Comparison (comparison table with retail context)
7. Growth Levers — What Moves the Needle (5-move strategy with content calendar)
8. This Week — Five Actions (immediate quick wins)
9. 90-Day Growth Timeline — From Shelf Brand to Story Brand (Tell the Story → Build the Bridge → Own the Shelf)
10. Brand Pulse Benchmarks (metrics table)
11. Brand Pulse Recheck Cadence
```

**Key framing for this format:** The core tension is "retail brand that happens to have an Instagram" vs. "story brand that happens to sell at retail." The shelf presence (Costco, Amazon, Whole Foods) should be leveraged as content — endcap walk-ups, sampling station footage, review-of-the-week Reels. Founders are foregrounded. Cultural IP (heritage, name etymology, family story) is treated as a moat. UGC pipelines and retail-to-social bridges are the growth levers.

## Portfolio Strategy

When Narin has audits for related accounts (e.g., a brand + its CEO + its scientific advisor), use complementary formats that highlight different dimensions:

| Account type | Recommended format | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand/company (health-tech, B2B-adjacent) | Competitive Battlefield | Market positioning is the story |
| Founder/CEO | Founder Story Arc | Personal narrative is the story |
| Academic/researcher | Research Translation Scorecard | Research credibility is the story |
| CPG/food/consumer brand | Brand Pulse Report | Retail-to-social bridge is the story |
The reader should feel: same analyst, different lens — not the same template applied three times.
