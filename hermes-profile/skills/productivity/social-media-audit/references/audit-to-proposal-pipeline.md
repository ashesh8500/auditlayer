# Audit-to-Proposal Pipeline

When a social media audit leads to a client engagement (as with @atllasai), the audit becomes the proposal. This reference covers converting audit findings into a structured social media management contract.

## The Pipeline

1. **Audit** → Identify weaknesses, content gaps, competitive position, brand voice opportunities
2. **Brand Voice Analysis** → Study the founder's personal accounts to extract vibe (see `brand-voice-analysis.md`)
3. **Content Strategy** → Convert weaknesses into content pillars with storytelling framework (see `storytelling-framework.md`)
4. **Proposal** → Build a 15-section contract outline using the template below

## Contract Structure (Standard Sections)

1. Parties & Term — 90-day minimum, 30-day notice, auto-renewing
2. Scope of Work — primary platform, services included, optional add-ons
3. Brand Voice — if applicable, document the founder's personal account vibe to replicate
4. Content Mix & Storytelling Commitment — pillars with percentages, storytelling lens applied
5. Weekly Content Calendar — day-by-day format assignment with story purpose
6. Deliverables & Approval Cadence — trust model on Reels/Stories, review on carousels
7. Tools & Subscriptions — client reimburses or directly provisions (CapCut, Canva, DaVinci Resolve, AuditLayer)
8. KPIs — baseline (from audit) vs 90-day targets with disclaimer
9. Pricing — 3 tiers (Core/Growth/Full-Stack) + setup fee + add-ons
10. Payment Terms — Net 15, 5% late fee, first month + setup due on signing
11. Client Responsibilities — assets, access, approvals, agent intros, point of contact
12. Content Ownership & Licensing — Narin owns until paid, client owns after, portfolio rights retained
13. Confidentiality — 2-year survival, standard exclusions
14. Termination — 30-day notice, immediate triggers for both parties
15. Limitation of Liability — cap at 3 months' fees, no guarantees on metrics
16. "What Makes This Different" — the closer, goes on the cover page

## Pricing Logic (Narin's Model)

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| Core | $2,500/mo | Strategy + calendar + 4 Reels + 1 carousel + captions + hashtags + cross-posting |
| Growth | $4,000/mo | Core + daily Stories + community management + monthly report |
| Full-Stack | $6,000/mo | Growth + TikTok management + YouTube Shorts management + LinkedIn + quarterly competitor audit |
| Setup | $2,000 one-time | Strategy doc, brand voice guide, content pillars, storytelling framework, hashtag bank, competitor dossier |

## Delivery Preferences

Narin prefers contracts shared directly in the chat (Telegram), not just as file attachments. When delivering:
1. Send the structured summary in chat first (sections 1-16 in message form)
2. Also save the .md file to `/home/asheshkaji/projects/narin/` for her records
3. The "What Makes This Different" section doubles as the proposal cover page copy

## Reel Volume: 4/Week (Not 5)

Narin's positioning: quality storytelling over volume. 4 Reels/week is the sweet spot — enough cadence for algorithmic favor, enough time to craft story arcs that match the founder's personal brand energy. More than 4 becomes a content treadmill; less than 3 loses momentum.

## The Audit IS the Proposal (Sales Psychology)

When pitching: the audit was done before the contract was discussed. This establishes:
- Credibility: "I already found everything broken"
- Specificity: "Here's exactly what needs to change, with numbers"
- Differentiation: no other social media manager leads with competitive intelligence

The proposal doesn't sell services — it sells the remediation plan for problems already diagnosed.
