# Content Ecosystem & Storytelling Frameworks for Social Strategy

These frameworks are used when building social media management proposals, content strategies, and contract outlines for brand/company accounts.

## The Storytelling Lens

Every piece of content must have three elements. If a post lacks any of these, it doesn't ship:

- **Hero** — Who is the protagonist? For B2B/SaaS: the customer, not the product. For founder-led brands: the founder. The AI is the sidekick, never the hero.
- **Villain** — What is the hero fighting? Missed calls. Dead leads. Manual dialing. Time wasted. Gatekeepers. Lost commissions. Burnout. The villain must be specific, felt, and named.
- **Transformation** — Before → After. "I used to do 200 dials/day. Now my AI qualifies leads while I'm at my kid's soccer game." Must include specific numbers and a human outcome.

## The Content Ecosystem Model

For accounts where the company has real people behind it (startups, small teams, founder-led brands):

| Role | Screen Time | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **CEO/Founder** | 60% — main face | Founder energy. Product vision. Building in public. Voice/personality. |
| **Team** | 25% — supporting cast | Engineers, sales, support. Real humans shipping product. Faces and names. |
| **Customers** | 15% — social proof | Testimonials. Transformations. Before/after. They're the heroes of their own story. |

**Rule:** "If it doesn't show a real human face, ask why not."

**Production model:** Weekly on-site shoot. Go to their office once a week, capture raw footage of everyone — Nima's day, team moments, office energy, product development, customer calls. This is the content factory. Everything else is edited from this footage.

## Translating Founder Brand to Company Account

When the founder has an existing personal brand (e.g., @neema_bardi — 10K IG, "filming my life until my startup hits $1M/month"), the company account (@atllasai) should channel the same energy, not invent a new corporate voice.

**What to study on the founder's account:**
- Bio language and tone
- Content formats (Reels vs carousels vs Stories ratio)
- Editing style (raw/CapCut vs polished/premiere)
- Vulnerability level (do they share struggles or only wins?)
- Personality markers (fitness, humor, late nights, specific phrases)
- Audience engagement style (do they reply? DM? Story polls?)

**What to port to the company account:**
- Same editing energy (if founder uses CapCut quick-cuts, company account uses same)
- Same vulnerability level (if founder shares struggles, company shares team struggles)
- Same voice (voice clone narration, first-person, "we" not "the company")
- Same content formats at different ratios (more product, less personal — but same style)
- Same rule: "If it feels like a polished ad, kill it. If it feels like something the founder would post, ship it."

## Content Mix Ratios (B2B SaaS / Startup Default)

| Pillar | % | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Product in Action | 35% | AI call demos, product walkthroughs — documentary style, not screencasts |
| Customer Transformations | 25% | Before/after stories. Real agents, real numbers, real faces. |
| Industry Narrative | 25% | "The old way is dead." Stats, competitor reactions, trends. Meme/voiceover format. |
| Founder / Team BTS | 15% | Building in public. Late nights, feature launches, team moments. |

**Weekly cadence (4 Reels + 1 carousel):**
- Mon: Customer transformation Reel
- Tue: Industry narrative carousel or Reel
- Wed: Product in action Reel
- Thu: Founder BTS or team spotlight Reel
- Fri: Product or agent story Reel
- Sat/Sun: Stories (call of the week, polls, competitor reactions)

## Contract / Proposal Structure

When building a social media management contract from an audit:

1. Parties & Term — 90-day minimum, 30-day notice
2. Scope of Work — platforms, formats, frequency, on-site shoots
3. Brand Voice — founder energy, ecosystem model, content rules
4. Content Mix & Storytelling — exact percentages, weekly calendar
5. Deliverables & Approval Cadence — trust model (no approval on Reels/Stories)
6. Tools & Subscriptions — client reimbursed or provisioned
7. KPIs — baseline from audit, 90-day targets with disclaimers
8. Pricing — tiered (Core/Growth/Full-Stack) + setup fee + travel stipend
9. Payment Terms — Net 15, late fee, first month + setup upfront
10. Non-Exclusivity — free to work with other clients, heads-up on competitors
11. Client Responsibilities
12. Content Ownership & Licensing
13. Confidentiality
14. Termination
15. Limitation of Liability
16. "What Makes This Different" — the closer section for the proposal cover page
