# Niche Calibration — Biohacking, Health & Wellness

Domain-specific calibration for auditing evidence-based creators in biohacking, health, and wellness. When auditing a creator in this niche, apply these calibrations on top of the standard framework.

---

## Content Format Priority

For biohacking/health/wellness creators, content effectiveness ranks:

1. **Podcasts** (highest conversion) — Long-form trust building. Mechanism deep-dives. The format where credentials do the heaviest lifting. A 90-minute episode with a PhD researcher converts better than 20 Reels. This is where biohacking audiences make purchasing decisions.

2. **Reels** (discovery) — Hook-driven science snippets. Protocol summaries. Myth-busting. Top of funnel. The algorithm favors Reels for reach, but the conversion path leads to podcasts.

3. **Bite-size paper breakdowns** (saves/credibility) — One study, one slide, one takeaway. Highest save rate. Builds credibility with the evidence-demanding segment. Not a growth lever on its own, but the trust signal that makes the growth levers work.

**Implication for audits:** When scoring content strategy, weight podcast presence and quality more heavily than Reel volume. A creator with no podcast but 5 Reels/week is missing their highest-converting format. A creator with a consistent podcast and fewer Reels may be making the right tradeoff.

## Audience Segments

The biohacking audience is not monolithic. When analyzing audience psychology and content gaps, identify which segment(s) the creator serves:

| Segment | Content They Want | Trust Signal |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| Quantified-self data nerds | Biomarker data, peer-reviewed citations, protocols | Study links, methodology transparency |
| Spiritual/energy healing | Integration of science + traditional wisdom | Respect for non-Western traditions, no dismissal |
| Performance athletes | VO2 max protocols, recovery science, mitochondrial health | Athletic credibility, personal results |
| Longevity/anti-aging | Lifespan extension, senescence research, supplement stacks | Research depth, nuanced risk discussion |
| Silicon Valley tech-bio | Wearable integration, nootropics, optimization culture | Tech fluency, data visualization |
| Biohacking moms | Family protocols, kids' nutrition, household detox | Practicality, safety-first framing |
| Clinicians (MD, NP, RN) | Clinical evidence, patient-applicable protocols | Clinical relevance, guideline awareness |
| PhD researchers | Deep mechanism dives, study design critique | Statistical rigor, limitations honestly stated |

**Calibration rule:** A creator serving quantified-self data nerds needs more citations per post than one serving spiritual/energy healing. Don't apply the same engagement quality standards across segments — a clinician audience may engage less but convert more.

## Credential Filter (Evidence-Based Biohacking)

AuditLayer's approach: only audit creators who can cite the paper behind the protocol. The credential filter excludes influencer-biohackers who repeat protocols without understanding root cause.

**Credentials that pass the filter:**
- PhD (any biomedical/health field)
- MD / DO
- NP (Nurse Practitioner)
- RD (Registered Dietitian)
- Researcher with verifiable publication record
- MSc with active research role

**Credentials that do NOT pass:**
- Self-taught biohacker (no formal credentials)
- Fitness influencer with biohacking interest
- Wellness coach without clinical/research background
- "Certified" from non-accredited programs

**Why this matters for audits:** Reports for credentialed creators should emphasize the trust signal that their credentials provide — it's a structural advantage that generic creators can't replicate. Frame monetization opportunities around their unique ability to interpret primary research for audiences.

## Engagement Rate Calibration

What "good" looks like differs by niche:

| Account Type | Good ER | Great ER | Exceptional ER |
|-------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| PhD researcher (science education) | 1.5–3% | 3–5% | 5%+ |
| MD clinician (patient education) | 2–4% | 4–7% | 7%+ |
| Wellness/lifestyle with credentials | 1.5–3% | 3–5% | 5%+ |
| Generic fitness influencer | 1–3% | 3–6% | 6%+ |

**Calibration rule:** Don't apply generic influencer ER benchmarks to credentialed accounts. A 2% ER on a PhD researcher's account is good — their audience is niche, their content is dense, and their conversion rate per engaged follower is higher.

## Monetization Pattern (Product-Owning Creators)

For creators who sell their own product (courses, test kits, consulting, supplements), the account is a **product discovery engine**, not a brand-deal vehicle. Do NOT estimate sponsored-post revenue — it's the wrong model.

Frame monetization as:
- Reach uplift from improved engagement → more product discovery → more conversions
- Content-to-product funnel analysis (how many posts before a product mention? Is the bridge clear?)
- Email list as the conversion asset (social → email → purchase)

Only estimate sponsored-post revenue if the user explicitly asks for it, and caveat that it's not the creator's actual business model.
