# 60/20/20 Creative Content Strategy

When the user asks for content ideas to support a specific account strategy, use this framework to generate original, purpose-built concepts — not repurposed existing assets.

## When to Use

- User asks "what type of content should we make?" after an audit
- User wants creative ideas that don't rely on existing footage, papers, or stage content
- User explicitly says "leave existing assets out" or "get creative"

## The Framework

### 60% — Reach-Building Content
Goal: attract new followers via algorithmic discovery. These are engineered for the non-follower feed.

Format principles:
- High hook rate in first 1.5 seconds
- High completion rate (keep it tight: 30–90 sec)
- High share velocity (people send these to friends)
- Text overlay throughout (85% watch without sound)

Creative approaches to generate ideas:
1. **"The [Cost/Impact] of…"** — Quantify everyday choices at the subject's domain level. One number, one visual, one takeaway. Series format.
2. **"A Day in the Life of…"** — Personify the subject's domain. Narrate from the perspective of the thing itself (mitochondria, a neuron, a cell). Narrative structure = high completion.
3. **Humor + Domain** — Fake text messages, first-person cellular perspectives, dry smart humor grounded in real knowledge. Screenshot-able, DM-shareable.
4. **"What I'd Tell My Younger Self"** — Wisdom format. Personal, vulnerable, direct to camera. Highest save rate on Instagram.
5. **"Trend or Reality?"** — Trend-jacking with domain expertise. Rate popular trends 1–10. Opinion triggers debate triggers comments triggers algorithm.
6. **Interactive Quiz** — Rapid-fire questions with pauses for the viewer to answer. Gamification triggers completion + comments simultaneously.
7. **"One Thing I Changed My Mind About"** — Intellectual humility. "I used to think X. Then I saw the data." Highest virality coefficient in science communication.
8. **"What Your [Authority Figure] Isn't Measuring"** — Education gap. Creates the need without selling the solution. High save rate.

### 20% — Product Education Content
Goal: convert educated followers into customers — without feeling like a product post.

Creative approaches:
1. **Personal testimony** — "The one test I run on myself." The subject uses their own product and shares what they learned.
2. **Self-assessment framing** — "Where do you fall on this spectrum?" Carousel that educates first, then offers the test as the natural next step.
3. **Problem-imagination content** — "What if you could track X like you track Y?" Plants desire before introducing the solution. No product shown.
4. **Unscripted FAQ** — Direct to camera, one real question per episode. Answers objections before they block purchase.

### 20% — Community Content
Goal: deepen relationships with existing followers. Build the comment-to-like ratio. Make followers feel like collaborators.

Creative approaches:
1. **Crowdsourced naming** — Ask followers to help name a new concept or protocol. Co-creation turns followers into evangelists.
2. **Honest personal share** — "The protocol I actually follow." Models imperfection. Invites sharing. Comment section becomes community.
3. **Analog Q&A** — Handwritten answers filmed on a whiteboard. Handwriting = intimacy. Stands out in a polished feed.
4. **Follower spotlight** — "This week, I learned from you." Share a comment that changed the subject's thinking. Closes the engagement loop.

## Through-Line

Every concept should feel like the subject's voice expressed through a format native to the platform — not academic or professional content translated onto it. Reach sparks curiosity. Product education solves the problem curiosity created. Community turns viewers into followers.
