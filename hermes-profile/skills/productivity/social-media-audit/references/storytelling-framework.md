# Storytelling Framework for Social Content

Every piece of content must have three elements. If it doesn't have all three, it doesn't ship.

## The Three Elements

| Element | Role | Example for B2B SaaS |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero** | The protagonist the audience identifies with | The real estate agent (not the AI, not the founder) |
| **Villain** | The pain point, obstacle, or status quo | Missed calls, dead leads, gatekeepers, manual dialing, burnout |
| **Transformation** | The before/after — specific and measurable | "200 dials/day → AI books 30 showings while agent sleeps" |

The product is never the hero. The product is the tool the hero uses to defeat the villain.

## Content Pillars × Storytelling Lens

Every content pillar gets a storytelling treatment:

| Pillar | Storytelling Angle | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Product in Action | "Watch this agent sleep through 30 calls and wake up to 5 booked appointments." AI is the sidekick. | 60-sec Reel, documentary-style |
| Agent Transformations | Before/after arc: "I used to _____. Now my AI does it." Real faces, real numbers. | Reaction-format Reel |
| Industry Narrative | "Cold calling is dead. Here's the data." Positioning as inevitable, not optional. | Carousel or green-screen Reel |
| Founder / Building in Public | Late nights, product updates, voice clone dev, "here's what we shipped." Raw and personal. | Voiceover Reel, @neema_bardi energy |

## Format Mapping

| Story Type | Best Format | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Transformation arc | 60-sec Reel | Full emotional journey fits the runtime |
| Before/after comparison | Carousel (5-10 slides) | Visual before/after, saveable |
| Product demo as story | Voice-note Reel | Actual AI call recording over captions |
| Founder reaction | Green-screen Reel | Reacting to customer wins, competitor claims |
| "Day in the life" | Split-screen Reel | Agent's day with AI vs without |
| "Call of the week" | Story series | One real AI call breakdown every week |

## Brand Voice Integration

When the client has a founder with a strong personal brand (e.g., Nima Bardi / @neema_bardi), the storytelling framework must channel that energy. See `brand-voice-analysis.md` for the full methodology.

Key principle: **"If it feels like a polished ad, kill it. If it feels like something the founder would post on their personal account, ship it."**
