Three lanes:
1. **Growing brands** — feature their audits → they repost → their followers see @auditlayer
2. **Peer collaborators** — small SMMs/strategists (5K-20K) for collab carousels
3. **Brand storytellers** — DTC case study accounts, snack reviewers for cross-promotion

## Finding micro-influencers (5K-20K)

Instagram-native methods (web search can't surface this tier):
1. **Hashtag mining:** Search #socialmediamanager, #brandstrategist, #contenttips, #cpgbrands, #snackreview — sort by Recent, check follower counts
2. **Similar accounts:** Instagram's suggested tab from @auditlayer
3. **Comment sections:** Go to larger strategist accounts (@brock11johnson, @sociallystef), look at thoughtful commenters, check their follower counts
4. **Startup CPG:** Follow @startupcpg — they tag small brands constantly

**Wellness & Beauty vertical:** For skincare, body care, oral care, hair care, femcare, and supplement brands in the 5K-20K range, apply the same methods but use beauty-focused hashtags: #indiebeauty, #cleanbeautybrand, #skincarefounder, #wellnessstartup, #beautyentrepreneur. Web search is even less reliable for this vertical — Instagram-native discovery is the only path for founder IGs.

## Calendar Post Execution — What Narin Wants

When Narin references a specific post from the 90-day calendar and asks for "examples" or a "breakdown," she wants the **slide-by-slide content with real Instagram accounts** she can screenshot. The abstract calendar entry is the brief — she needs the execution.

**Deliverable format:**
- Slide-by-slide breakdown with caption text for each slide
- **Real account names** for both good and bad examples
- **Bad examples should be anonymized** (crop handles/logos, blur profile pics) — the audience should recognize the PATTERN, not the specific brand
- **Good examples can be named** with handles — they're aspirational role models
- Include the specific hook/data callout text for each slide
- Tell her *what* to screenshot (profile grid, individual post, comments section)

**Clarify which post:** When she says "the Monday post" or "examples from the calendar," she usually means the most recently discussed or most imminent calendar entry. Before going broad, confirm which specific calendar entry she's referring to. Listing all 12 Monday posts when she wants one is a wasted turn.

**Verified accounts as examples:** When hunting for accounts to use as slide examples, web search works well for finding accounts by category (small CPG brands, inconsistent feeds, etc.). But individual Instagram post URLs from brands are nearly invisible to search engines — Instagram's index surfaces influencer/UGC content, not brand-owned posts. Once you have an account handle, use `browser_navigate` to individual post URLs to verify content. The "More posts from [user]" carousel on any post page gives 6–12 more URLs.
