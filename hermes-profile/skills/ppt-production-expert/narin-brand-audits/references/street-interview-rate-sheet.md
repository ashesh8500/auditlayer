### The Approach

Conversational, person-on-the-street. No clipboard energy. I act as both interviewer and someone who genuinely knows the science — because I do. Questions are designed to open people up, not box them in, and [product] is introduced naturally in conversation rather than as a hard pitch. The science sells itself when explained by someone who understands it.

**Sample question arc (10 questions):**

1. "When you hear the word mitochondria, what comes to mind?"
2. "Be honest — have you thought about your cells today?"
3. "When you feel tired, where do you think that tiredness lives?"
4. "What do you do to boost your energy?"
5. "Do you ever think about your energy at a cellular level?"
6. "If you could measure your body's energy production — like a fuel gauge — would you want to know?"
7. "[Product intro question — what's your reaction to this?]"
8. "[Science pedigree question — does knowing the origins change how you feel?]"
9. "[Price reaction question — does that feel worth it?]"
10. "What would it change for your life to know your [metric]?"
