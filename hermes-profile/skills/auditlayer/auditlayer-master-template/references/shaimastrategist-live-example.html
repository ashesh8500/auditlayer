4. Weaknesses

538% Following-to-Follower Ratio

This is the single largest structural problem on the account. Instagram's classifier interprets a ratio above 10–15% as a low-trust signal. At 538%, the algorithm caps initial test-audience reach on every post before the 4-hour engagement window even opens. No content strategy can outperform this penalty — it must be fixed first.

Only 15 Posts — Not a Portfolio

A social media strategist selling strategy services needs a body of work. 15 posts is not enough to demonstrate range, consistency, or results. A potential client landing on this profile sees a strategist who doesn't post — which undermines the entire value proposition.

Zero Reels

Reels are Instagram's primary discovery engine, accounting for over 50% of non-follower reach on the platform. Shaima has published zero Reels — meaning her account is invisible to anyone not already following her. For a rebrand trying to reach new audiences, this is a critical gap.

No Defined Niche

Skincare + wellness + perfumes + AI video + adverts = five audiences with different expectations. A strategist who works with "everyone" competes with every other generalist freelancer on the platform. The algorithm cannot identify a clear audience to serve her content to, and potential clients don't immediately know if she's right for their brand.

No Lead Generation Path

The bio ends with an email address and a perfume brand link. There is no freebie, no lead magnet, no CTA to DM, no portfolio link — nothing that converts a profile visitor into a warm lead. Every profile visit that leaves without taking an action is a missed client opportunity.

No Highlights Structure

Instagram Highlights are prime real estate — they sit directly below the bio and are the second thing a visitor sees. Shaima has no Highlights, which means a visitor must scroll the (thin) feed to understand what she offers. Most won't.

Fix the Following Ratio — This Week

8

"Hot take: [industry opinion everyone's afraid to say]"
