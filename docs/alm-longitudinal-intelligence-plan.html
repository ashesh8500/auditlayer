AuditLayerMedia · Product reset · 22 July 2026

From one-off audits to longitudinal intelligence.

A targeted plan to repair submission and waiting first, then give every brand, creator, person, or organization a durable identity, evidence history, score lineage, and recommendation memory—without turning report generation into an open-ended agent loop.

Diagnosis

The report is currently the product’s center of gravity. It should be an output of a durable subject state, not the only place where the system “remembers.”

Proposed center

Subject → Living Brief → Channels → Evidence → Decisions → Audits. Reports become time-stamped, evidence-backed views over that system.

2. Target product model

Use Subject as the neutral internal/product object. A subject may be a brand, person, creator, organization, or project. A subject owns zero or more channels. A one-off public target may remain “observed” and must not appear as managed workspace property until connected or explicitly promoted.

The visible continuity artifact

Working name: Living Brief. It is more inclusive than “Brand Identity” and less technical than “memory.” Internally it may export as LIVING_BRIEF.md, but the source of truth is validated, versioned structured data.

The current brief contains:

  • Subject type and confirmed identity
  • Vision and intent
  • Audience and offers
  • Voice and positioning
  • Current goals and success criteria
  • Constraints and non-goals
  • Active experiments and planned changes
  • User-confirmed strategic decisions
Editing rule: every user edit creates a version. The agent may propose a diff, but it may not silently rewrite vision, identity, or goals.

3. The redesigned user journeys

New audit

  1. Choose a subject. Show recent managed subjects, connected channels, prior observed targets, and “Create a new subject.”
  2. Choose channels. Multi-select existing Instagram/web/etc. channels or paste URLs. Platform selection is explicit when detection is uncertain.
  3. Confirm the lens. Choose a strategic goal and show the current Living Brief summary. Ask “What has changed since the last audit?” rather than a generic context box.
  4. Build a batch. “Add another” creates multiple audit requests. Before submission, show count, entitlement/credit effect, duplicates, estimated duration, and selected report types.
  5. Submit atomically. One idempotent transaction creates the batch and its audits or creates none.

Waiting

Replace eleven developer phases with three customer states:

Customer stateMeaningCustomer copy
PreparingAccepted, entitlement checked, evidence plan built.“Preparing your evidence run.”
AnalyzingRequired sources are being refreshed and analysis is running.“Checking current signals and comparing them with your previous state.”
FinalizingValidation, deterministic scoring, rendering, and storage.“Verifying evidence links and assembling your report.”
  • No cache hit, event type, actor, heartbeat, realtime status, worker name, retry number, or raw event detail is client-visible.
  • The customer may leave immediately. The page says this explicitly and offers in-app/email completion notification.
  • A server-owned terminal status—not missing timeline events—stops animation.
  • A watchdog turns stale work into Delayed with a calm recovery message and an operator incident. No infinite spinner.
  • Founder diagnostics move to admin-only incident/event surfaces.
Security boundary: customer progress is an allowlisted projection, never a filtered copy of internal logs.

4. Evidence, citations, scores, and continuity

Evidence records

Stop caching one research paragraph. Store addressable evidence items with stable IDs such as IG#profile-20260722, WEB#official-about-7f1a, CTX#brief-v4, and METH#score-v2.

FieldPurpose
source_type / source_urlAuthority and citation target.
observed_at / expires_atTruthful freshness; reuse never renews observation time.
content_hashDetect change without reinterpreting unchanged sources.
confidence / coverageSupport cautious claims and absence checks.
subject_id / channel_id / audit_idPrevent cross-subject contamination.

Score contract

Keep observable dimension scores separate from the audit’s perspective. A Living Brief should alter weights, thresholds, and action priority, not allow the model to freely invent different facts.

  • Each dimension score stores its method version, evidence IDs, rationale, confidence, and observation date.
  • The overall score is computed locally from versioned weights.
  • Every score is clickable. An evidence drawer shows what raised or lowered it.
  • Unavailable evidence renders “Data needed,” never a punitive zero.
  • A material score change requires one classified reason: evidence changed, brief/lens changed, methodology changed, or prior error corrected.
  • Cross-method comparisons display a comparability warning rather than implying progress.

Recommendation ledger

Recommendations become stable objects with states: proposed, accepted, rejected, in_progress, implemented, deferred, superseded, or invalidated. Each links to findings and evidence.

Every repeat audit must answer:

  1. What changed?
  2. What stayed true?
  3. What advice was acted on?
  4. Why did any recommendation or score change?

5. Give Hermes a bounded, useful job

The critical path should be a deterministic state machine. Hermes should provide constrained judgment inside it, not control the process.

  1. Continuity compiler: load latest Living Brief, accepted decisions, active recommendations, previous scorecard, and source freshness.
  2. Hermes delta planner: return a small JSON research plan. It may add at most a bounded number of optional queries; deterministic freshness rules may require sources regardless.
  3. Tool executor: run required searches/fetches in parallel outside the agent. Fresh connected metrics are mandatory.
  4. Typed analyst: one DeepSeek V4 Flash call produces findings, score inputs, actions, and change explanations referencing only valid evidence IDs.
  5. Local gate: resolve citations, compute scores, reject contradictions/unsupported claims, render deterministically, and atomically persist the snapshot and ledgers.
  6. Learning proposal: after success, Hermes may propose a Living Brief or recommendation-state diff. The client confirms identity/vision changes.
Memory rule: canonical state lives in the database. A subject-scoped HERMES_HOME is rebuildable working state, keyed by subject UUID—not user UUID—and never outranks the ledger.

6. Targeted deliverables

P0 · Review gate

Repair submission and waiting

Scope: existing/connected subject suggestions, explicit platform cards, previous-target autofill, multi-audit batch review, customer-safe three-state progress, no event log, terminal-state spinner fix, stale-run watchdog.

Why first: this is what an external reviewer and a second-time customer touch immediately.

Acceptance: an incognito reviewer can create one or several audits without retyping known handles; leave the page; return to accurate terminal state; and never see an internal event detail.

P1 · Ontology

Introduce Subject, Channels, and Living Brief

Scope: migrate managed/connected accounts into subject/channel relationships; preserve one-off report targets as observed; add versioned Living Brief and a subject home; link every new audit to a subject and pinned brief version.

Acceptance: one brand or creator can hold Instagram plus website without duplication; observed celebrity targets do not appear as managed properties; historical reports remain accessible.

P2 · Truth layer

Ship evidence-linked score and recommendation ledgers

Scope: immutable evidence snapshots, evidence items, dimension score rows, findings, recommendations, decisions, citations, evidence drawer, “since last audit,” and contradiction validation.

Acceptance: every factual score rationale resolves to evidence; rejected advice does not reappear without new evidence; implemented advice is acknowledged; score movement has an explicit cause.

P3 · Runtime

Replace open-ended in-process generation with the locked delta pipeline

Scope: subject-scoped state, deterministic continuity compiler, bounded Hermes research planner, external parallel tool execution, one typed analysis call, deterministic scoring/rendering/commit, per-source freshness.

Acceptance: repeat audits skip unchanged exploration, refresh required volatile data, remain more coherent than first audits, and beat the current p50/p95 latency without quality regression.

P4 · Feedback

Close the self-learning loop safely

Scope: client/founder recommendation states, Living Brief change proposals, correction scope (subject/niche/global), policy promotion review, and retention surfaces.

Acceptance: the system learns from explicit decisions without silently mutating identity or leaking one subject’s corrections into another.

7. Suggested data boundary

subjects subject_channels subject_brief_versions subject_brief_proposals audit_batches audits.subject_id audits.brief_version_id audits.evidence_snapshot_id audits.methodology_version evidence_snapshots evidence_items audit_scores audit_findings subject_recommendations recommendation_evidence subject_decisions client_audit_status -- allowlisted public projection operator/audit_events -- private diagnostics only

Use additive migrations and backfill before readers switch. Existing report artifacts and report-version rows remain immutable. Retire accounts.research_snapshot only after evidence-item reads are live.

8. Tests that define “resolved”

  • Previous subjects and channels appear in New Audit.
  • Platform ambiguity requires explicit confirmation.
  • A batch is atomic and idempotent.
  • Duplicate active audits are intercepted.
  • Customer API never returns internal event details.
  • Ready/failed/delayed always stops animation.
  • Stale heartbeat creates an operator incident.
  • One person owns multiple channels under one subject.
  • Observed target is excluded from managed workspace.
  • Living Brief edits create immutable versions.
  • Every score evidence ID resolves.
  • Unavailable dimensions do not become zero.
  • Method changes prevent false score comparison.
  • Rejected recommendation is not repeated.
  • Implemented recommendation appears as progress.
  • Fresh connected metric failure fails closed.
  • Cache reuse preserves original observation time.
  • Unchanged low-volatility source is not re-explored.
  • Subject A never enters Subject B’s context.
  • Old report versions remain retrievable.

9. Decisions and non-decisions

Decide nowSubject is the parent; channels are children; reports are outputs.
Decide nowLiving Brief is editable and versioned; agent changes are proposed diffs.
Decide nowCustomer progress is a separate allowlisted surface, not audit logs.
Decide nowEvidence freshness is per item/source, not one sliding blob TTL.
Decide nowHermes plans bounded deltas; deterministic code controls execution and writes.
Can waitFinal feature name. “Living Brief” is the strongest working name; alternatives can be tested in product copy after the object exists.
Do not doDo not build a graph database, self-hosted crawler fleet, vector memory layer, or multi-agent report swarm for this release.

10. Recommended implementation order

Approve P0–P3 as one product direction, but release them as four independently reversible vertical slices. P0 should precede Meta review. P1 creates the durable product foundation. P2 makes the analysis defensible. P3 then removes the latency and amnesia caused by giving the agent responsibility before giving it structured state.

Smallest coherent first move: P0 plus the additive audit_batches and customer-safe progress projection. Do not start by renaming Accounts or adding more memory fields.
